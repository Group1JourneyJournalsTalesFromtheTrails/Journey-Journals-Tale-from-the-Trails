Rizal Shrine

INFORMATION SECTION

  • Brief History:

    The Rizal Shrine is a typical rectangular Bahay na Bato, reminiscent of upper-class Filipino homes built during the Spanish Colonial era. The lower portion is made of adobe stone and brick, while the upper portion consists of hardwood. The shrine has a large lawn with a replica of a nipa hut Rizal used to play with his sisters in. Next to the hut is a statue of a young Jos√© Rizal, with his dog. This statue was constructed in 1996 for the centennial celebration commemorating Rizal's death.


  • Recommended Activities

    Ancestral Home:
    The meticulously reconstructed "Bahay na Bato" (stone house) offers a glimpse into Rizal's 19th-century upbringing. Admire the period furniture, artwork, and artifacts, many of which belonged to the Rizal family. Imagine young Jose playing in the azotea (balcony) or studying in his room.
  • Explore the museum:
    Delve deeper into Rizal's life and work through the museum's extensive collection. See his writings, paintings, sculptures, and personal belongings, including his medical instruments and travel trunks. Learn about his political activism, literary pursuits, and tragic execution.
  • Witness a cultural show:
    Immerse yourself in Filipino tradition by attending a cultural show hosted by the shrine. Enjoy vibrant performances of folk dances like the Tinikling and Itik-Itik, accompanied by melodious kundiman music. Gain appreciation for the rich cultural heritage that shaped Rizal's identity.
